SC/ST Case Management System

About the System

The SC/ST Case Management System is a comprehensive digital platform designed to streamline the monitoring and resolution of cases registered under the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989. This system ensures efficient handling of cases from registration to final disposal while maintaining transparency and accountability.

Key Features

Case Registration & Tracking

  • Online FIR registration for SC/ST atrocity cases
  • Real-time case status tracking for victims and officials
  • Automated alerts at every stage of case progression
  • Unique case identification numbers for easy reference

Compensation Management

  • Automated calculation of compensation amounts based on offence type
  • Disbursement tracking with payment milestones
  • Integration with bank systems for direct benefit transfer
  • Monitoring of rehabilitation measures

Monitoring & Reporting

  • Dashboard for district and state-level monitoring
  • Automated reminders for pending cases
  • Generation of statutory reports for SC/ST Commissions
  • Analytics for identifying atrocity patterns

System Workflow

  1. Case Registration: FIR filed at police station or online portal
  2. Verification: Designated officer verifies case details
  3. Initial Relief: Immediate compensation disbursed as per norms
  4. Investigation: Dedicated investigating officer assigned
  5. Chargesheet: System tracks submission deadlines
  6. Court Process: Trial monitoring with hearing dates
  7. Final Relief: Remaining compensation upon conviction
  8. Rehabilitation: Tracking of long-term support measures

System Benefits

  • Reduces manual paperwork and delays
  • Ensures timely justice delivery
  • Prevents case pendency and oversight
  • Maintains complete case history
  • Provides transparency to victims
  • Facilitates data-driven policy making
  • Enables quick access to case documents
  • Standardizes compensation process

Stakeholders

Who Uses This System?

  • Victims/Complainants: To register cases and track progress
  • Police Departments: For FIR registration and investigation
  • District Administration: For monitoring and compensation
  • Social Welfare Officers: For rehabilitation measures
  • Prosecutors: For case preparation and court processes
  • SC/ST Commissions: For oversight and reporting

Legal Framework

This system implements the provisions of:

  • Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989
  • SC/ST (PoA) Amendment Act, 2015
  • SC/ST (PoA) Rules, 1995 & Amendment Rules, 2016
  • State Government notifications on compensation
