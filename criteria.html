Criteria for Filing SC/ST Atrocity Cases

Important Note:

The Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 provides special protections and procedures for filing cases of atrocities against SC/ST communities. Below are the key criteria and conditions.

Who Can File a Complaint

  1. The victim - Any member of SC/ST community who has faced discrimination or violence
  2. Family members - Parents, siblings, spouse, or children of the victim
  3. Witnesses - Any person who has witnessed the atrocity
  4. Authorized representatives - Social workers or organization representatives with written authorization
  5. Police officers - Can register cases suo moto (on their own motion)
View Official Guidelines

Types of Atrocities Covered

The Act specifies various offenses that qualify as atrocities, including but not limited to:

  • Forcing to eat/drink inedible substances
  • Dumping excreta/waste matter in premises
  • Removing clothes or parading naked
  • Sexual exploitation or assault
  • Denial of access to public places
  • Forced labor or begging
  • False attribution of offenses
  • Malicious prosecution
  • Economic boycott
  • Social boycott

Where to File a Complaint

  1. Police Station - Nearest police station (must register FIR immediately)
  2. Special Courts - Designated courts for SC/ST cases
  3. Human Rights Commissions - National or State commissions
  4. Online Portals - National SC/ST Hub or state-specific portals
  5. District Magistrate - Can directly receive complaints

Required Documentation

While filing a complaint, you should provide (if available):

  • Written complaint with details of incident
  • Date, time and location of incident
  • Names and descriptions of perpetrators
  • Names of witnesses
  • Medical reports (in case of physical violence)
  • Photographs/videos/audio recordings
  • Caste certificate of victim
Download Complaint Form

Time Limits and Procedures

  1. No time limit - Can be filed at any time after the incident
  2. Immediate FIR - Police must register case immediately
  3. Investigation - Must be completed within 60 days
  4. Special Court - Trial should preferably conclude within 2 months
  5. Compensation - Interim relief should be provided within 7 days
