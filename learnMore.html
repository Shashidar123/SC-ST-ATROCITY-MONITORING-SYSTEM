SC/ST Atrocities Relief Scheme - Andhra Pradesh

Government Order: G.O.Ms.No.95

Dated: 29.08.2016

Department: Social Welfare (CV.POA) Department

This order enhances compensation amounts for SC/ST victims of atrocities under the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989.

Compensation Details

Key Features

  • Compensation ranges from ₹85,000 to ₹8,25,000 depending on the nature of offence
  • Payment is made in installments at different stages of case proceedings
  • Additional relief measures including housing, education, and employment for severe cases
  • Effective from 14.04.2016 (date of Central Government's notification)

Compensation Structure

Sl. No. Name of the Offence Relief Amount Payment Schedule
1-5 Putting inedible substances, dumping excreta, garlanding with footwear, etc. ₹1,00,000 25% at FIR, 50% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
6-7 Wrongful occupation/dispossession of land ₹1,00,000 + restoration 25% at FIR, 50% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
8-11 Forced labor, manual scavenging, devadasi system ₹1,00,000 25% at FIR, 50% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
12-16 Preventing voting, false legal proceedings ₹85,000 25% at FIR, 50% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
23 Sexual harassment of SC/ST women ₹2,00,000 25% at FIR, 50% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
24 Acid attacks ₹85,000 to ₹8,25,000 (based on severity) 50% at FIR, 50% after medical report
44 Rape or Gang rape ₹8,25,000 50% after medical report, 25% at chargesheet, 25% at conviction
45 Murder or Death ₹8,25,000 50% after post mortem, 50% at chargesheet

Additional Relief Measures

For victims of murder, death, massacre, rape, gang rape, permanent incapacitation:

  • Pension of ₹5,000 per month to dependents
  • Employment to one family member
  • Provision of agricultural land/house if needed
  • Full education cost up to graduation level for children
  • Provision of food supplies for 3 months
  • Reconstruction of burnt/destroyed houses at government cost

Implementation

Responsible Authorities

  • Director of Social Welfare, Andhra Pradesh
  • Commissioner of Tribal Welfare, Andhra Pradesh
  • District Collectors & Magistrates
  • Superintendents of Police

All authorities are directed to implement the Act and Rules scrupulously and ensure wide publicity through print and electronic media.

Legal Basis

This order implements the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) (Amendment) Rules, 2016 notified by the Government of India on 14.04.2016.

For assistance or to report violations, contact your nearest Social Welfare Office or District Collector's office.
