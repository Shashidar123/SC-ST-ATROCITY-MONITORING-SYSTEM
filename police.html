Rules & Guidelines (GMOs)

The following rules and guidelines are issued by Government Monitoring Organizations (GMOs) through National Informatics Centre (NIC) for filing SC/ST atrocity cases:

The Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989

  • The Act prohibits commission of offences against members of SC/ST communities
  • Provides for special courts for trial of such offences and relief for victims
  • Mandates immediate registration of FIR for offences under the Act
  • Provides for rights of victims and witnesses

Eligibility for Filing Complaint

  • Victim or family member of victim belonging to SC/ST community
  • Any person who has knowledge of the offence can file a complaint
  • Public servant who comes to know about the offence in discharge of official duties

Required Documents

  • Identity proof of complainant (Aadhaar, Voter ID, etc.)
  • Caste certificate of victim (if available)
  • Any evidence related to the incident (photos, videos, medical reports, etc.)
  • Witness statements (if available)

Procedure After Filing

  • FIR must be registered immediately (within 24 hours)
  • Investigation must be completed within 60 days
  • Charge sheet must be filed within 60 days of FIR registration
  • Trial must be completed within 2 months from filing of charge sheet

Relief and Rehabilitation Measures

  • Immediate relief in cash or kind to victims
  • Payment of transportation costs for medical attention
  • Maintenance allowance for victim and dependents
  • Pension to widows or dependents in case of death
